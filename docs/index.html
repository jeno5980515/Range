Fried's Notes & Sizes for Each Chart Grouping

RFI

RFI to 2.25bb.

RFI frequencies by position:

  • UTG: 17.5%
  • MP: 20.8%
  • CO: 29.41%
  • BTN: 43.74%
  • SB: 43.74%

vs RFI

Playing a "3-bet or fold" strategy from HJ, CO, and SB.

3-bet sizes vs RFI:

  • 3-bet to 7.25bb IP as HJ/CO/BTN.
  • 3-bet to 9.5bb as BB vs SB.
  • 3-bet to 9.75bb as SB.
  • 3-bet to 11.25bb as BB OOP.

BB vs other sizes: 3-bet range stays fixed. Calling ranges for vs 3bb, 2.5bb, 2.25bb, and 2bb are included.

Squeeze & After Squeeze

IP: squeeze to 9.75bb, 4-bet to 24.25bb.

OOP: squeeze to 12.25bb, 4-bet to 26.25bb.

BTN vs HJ RFI & CO Call: BTN only ended up with a 1% calling range in the sim, so I simplified the range to squeeze or fold.

BONUS: a simplified version of a chart from Kanu7's Advanced Cash Game Strategy course with more BTN calls. As my sim had so few calls, I wanted to add a chart that shows what a range with more calls could look like.

No SB ranges are included, as I recommend a 3-bet or fold strategy from the SB. However, the module does have a brief look at SB play vs a squeeze

General SB advice: SB will still play 3-bet or fold when facing an RFI and a call. Squeeze slightly tighter than your regular 3-bet range vs the RFI without an overcaller.

Blind vs Blind

Recommend RFI or fold (no limp) in SB.

Raise sizes:

  • RFI to 3bb
  • BB 3-bet to 9.5bb
  • SB 4-bet to 25.5bb (or 4-bet shove)

BONUS: SB limped. My sim uses a BB isolation raise to 4bb, SB limp-reraise to 14, BB 4-bet to 29bb (or shove). This leads to a more polarized response than you might see in sims that raise to 3bb vs the SB open-limp. More details are in the videos.

RFI vs 3-Bet

Raise sizes:

  • HJ/CO/BTN 3-bet to 7.25bb, 4-bet to 23.25bb
  • SB 3-bets to 9.75bb, 4-bet to 23.25bb
  • BB 3-bets OOP to 11.25bb, 4-bet to 25.25bb
  • BB 3-bets vs SB to 9.5bb, 4-bet to 25.5bb

UTG vs HJ/CO: grouped together as they are very similar. Discussed in the videos.

vs 3-Bet Cold

Recommended to play 4-bet or fold (no calling range) facing an RFI and a 3-bet.

  • Cold 4-bet in position to 19.75bb (after 2.25 RFI -> 7.25 3B).
  • Cold 4-bet to 23.25bb OOP

Overall range recommendations: Rather under-bluff than over-bluff in this situation. 

Same recommended 4-bet range from different spots vs a certain opener and 3-bettor. Maybe play a bit tighter as SB compared to BB.

vs 4-Bet

In my sim, 4-bets are bigger (23.25-25.5bb) than in some other sims.

Should you face smaller 4-bets (say you 3-bet to 7.25 and face a 20bb 4-bet):

  • You need to call a bit wider preflop with some extra suited hands.
  • Shoving becomes a less attractive option, so you’ll call more in position with hands like JJ-QQ and AKo.
  • AKs & KK always shove preflop.
  • AA becomes a slow-play more often.

vs 4-bet shove: The sim doesn’t have 4-bet shove options in every spot. The situations where there’s a 4bet shove are covered in those spots.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is RFI, vs RFI, Vs 3-Bet Cold, etc? Expand
  • RFI (short for Raise First In): Use these charts when you are the first player to enter the pot preflop.
  • vs RFI: Use these charts when a player in front of you has raised.
  • Squeeze & After Squeeze: Use these charts when a player in front of you has raised and another player has called, plus situations that occur from there.
  • RFI vs 3-Bet: Use these charts when you are faced with a 3-bet after raising first in.
  • vs 3-Bet Cold+: Use these charts when a player in front of you has raised and another player has 3-bet before your action, plus situations that occur from there.
  • vs 4-Bet: Use these charts when you are faced with a 4-bet after 3-betting.
  • Blind vs Blind: Use these charts when the action folds to the blinds.
How were these ranges created? Expand

As Fried explains in the module that came out with these charts, the underlying ranges were created using Monker Solver with the following inputs:

  • No calling in HJ/CO/SB.
  • 5% rake with a cap of 0.6bb (500NL Zoom rake).
  • Sizes: 2.25 RFI -> 7.25 3-bet -> 23.25 4-bet.
  • 3-bet to 9.75bb from SB, 11.25bb from BB.

Fried then took the outputted ranges and went over them thoroughly, simplifying the overall strategy and mixed frequencies to make the charts easier to use in practice.
